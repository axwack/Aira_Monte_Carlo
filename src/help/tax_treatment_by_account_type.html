The critical issue with the advice you received

"Spend taxable first" — you have no taxable brokerage account. Every dollar you own is in Solo 401k (pre-tax), Roth IRA, or HSA. The 0% LTCG opportunity at $98,900 MFJ does not apply to any of your current accounts. Pre-tax 401k withdrawals are always ordinary income — there is no capital gains treatment available, no matter how long you held VOO inside it.
NJ kills the 0% LTCG advantage anyway. NJ taxes capital gains as ordinary income — no preferential rate. A MFJ couple at $98,900 income in NJ pays 0% federal LTCG but ~6.37% NJ on those same gains. FL or Thailand domicile = true 0% (federal + state). This is why your domicile decision before retirement is your single highest-leverage tax move.
Your actual highest-tax outcome: Pre-tax Solo 401k withdrawals in NJ at retirement — ~22–24% federal + ~6.37% NJ = ~28–30% combined on every dollar. Your Roth conversion ladder targets this exact problem.
Your actual lowest-tax outcome: Roth IRA withdrawals anywhere = 0% federal, 0% NJ, no NIIT, no RMD, no state. Then HSA (medical). Then Airbnb income (partially sheltered by depreciation). Then SS (partially excluded). Pre-tax 401k is last resort in the withdrawal waterfall.
